A Slow Dawn

I remember, in those early years,
we spoke with earnest hearts —
one word was one truth.

At dawn, by the train station,
the long street still dark, no footsteps yet.
A small soybean-milk shop breathing warm steam.

Back then, sunlight moved slowly.
Carriages and letters took their time.
A lifetime was only enough to love one person.

Even locks were beautiful —
keys crafted with tenderness and form.
When you locked the door,
the world understood.
